# Recommendations for Improving Prosthetic Care Access

Based on the findings from ClinicalTrials.gov analysis and OpenStreetMap provider mapping across three underserved regions (Rural West Virginia, Appalachian Kentucky, Mississippi Delta), the following recommendations are proposed:

## Immediate Actions (0-6 months)

### 1. Establish Mobile Prosthetic/Orthotic Clinics
- Deploy rotating prosthetic fitting and maintenance clinics to the three target regions
- Partner with existing rural health clinics (e.g., Community Care of Green Bank, Leflore County Health Center) as hosts
- Target: 1 mobile clinic per region, serving ~50 patients/month

### 2. Teleprosthetics Pilot Program
- Launch telehealth consultations for prosthetic/orthotic fittings and adjustments
- Partner with prosthetists from regions with existing expertise (e.g., Ottobock Göttingen, German prosthetic research centers)
- Provide subsidized digital imaging equipment to rural clinics for remote fitting consultations

### 3. Clinical Trial Recruitment Partnerships
- **381 actively recruiting prosthetic trials** represent an untapped resource
- Partner with trial sites to offer travel stipends or mobile trial units for underserved participants
- Prioritize trials focused on lower-limb prosthetics and orthotic rehabilitation

## Medium-Term Actions (6-18 months)

### 4. Prosthetic Training Pipeline
- Establish prosthetic/orthotic technician training programs at community colleges in each region
- Partner with existing physical therapy programs (e.g., Tri-State Physical Therapy, Marden Rehabilitation)
- Target: Graduate 10-15 prosthetic technicians per year per region

### 5. Pharmacy-to-Prosthetic Referral Network
- Leverage existing pharmacy infrastructure (28-42 pharmacies across regions) as referral points
- Train pharmacists to identify prosthetic/orthotic need and provide basic information
- Create standardized referral cards for pharmacy distribution

### 6. Data Infrastructure
- Build a real-time provider directory specific to prosthetic/orthotic services
- Integrate with Medicaid/Medicare databases to identify uninsured prosthetic patients
- Create mapping dashboard for ongoing gap monitoring

## Long-Term Actions (18-36 months)

### 7. Permanent Regional Prosthetic Centers
- Establish 1-2 permanent prosthetic/orthotic centers per region
- Co-locate with existing hospitals or large clinics to share infrastructure
- Hire local staff from newly trained technicians

### 8. Research-to-Care Translation
- Leverage the 2,383 US-based prosthetic clinical trials to inform best practices
- Create a formal channel for translating trial findings into rural clinical protocols
- Partner with prosthetics researchers (e.g., Sanders JE group, Ottobock research)

### 9. Policy Advocacy
- Use this gap analysis to advocate for:
  - Medicare/Medicaid prosthetic coverage expansion in rural areas
  - Federal grants for prosthetic training programs in underserved regions
  - Telecom infrastructure improvements to support teleprosthetics

## Conclusion

The data reveals a critical disconnect: the US produces nearly half of global prosthetic research yet has zero serving 3.3 million people in the most underserved regions. Addressing this gap requires coordinated action across mobile care, teleprosthetics, workforce development, and policy reform. This atlas provides the evidentiary foundation for that action.
